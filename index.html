Meet Catherine Crump

Catherine Crump is a professor of law at UC Berkeley, where she directs the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic. Her work sits at the intersection of technology, government power, and civil liberties.

She studies how new surveillance tools — from drones and automated license-plate readers to cell-phone tracking and police body cameras — reshape privacy, free speech, and the balance of power between people and the state. Before Berkeley, she was a staff attorney at the ACLU, litigating cutting-edge First and Fourth Amendment cases.

Civil Liberties in 60 Seconds is her series of short, plain-language videos that make constitutional rights and surveillance news accessible to everyone.
